    Iceland - June 2013

    We travelled around iceland for about 2 weeks. It was a trip to never forget. Iceland really is a beautifull country and overwhelmed us sometimes by it's astonishing nature. In this post we will try to describe our experiences about our trip to share it with the world

    We saw about 68 big trucks on the road during the whole 2 weeks, added about 40 small trucks to that comes to a total of 128 trucks we crossed on the roads. Just to give an impression of the traffic on the island.

    We booked our flight with connections in one of their offices at Antwerp. Mainly because the overflood of information on the internet about flight tickets and because the people their also know what the difference in price is by changing hours etc for your flight.

    For some weeks we were quite clueless about a renting car. Should we rent a 4x4 drive, a normal car during the 2 weeks or only one week or? After some talks with friends already travelled in Iceland and looking up the prices of a 4x4 drive against a normal car we decided to rent a normal car via sunnycars for about 800 euro the 2 weeks.

    Day 1

    Our flight departed in Brussels National airport, we took the train from Antwerp to Brussels using the diablo express. Suprisingly we didn't suffered any delays. Once in the airport we had to wait a bit to check in our luggage. Mainly because a group of people tried to check in some special lugage as bikes and stuff.

    Nevertheless everything went smooth and we arrived one time in the Keflavik Airport by Iceland Air. (Using the planefinder you can track down your flight)

    We got a toyota yaris after filling in the paper work with a friendly lady by Hertz at the airport and started on the first of 2968 km's we drove around Iceland. Since we were hungry the end of that first km was the centre of Keflavik where we stopped at Kaffi Duus and ate both a good tasting pasta enyoing our view over the Keflavik harbour.

    After we regained some energy we drove to Gardur, where we did some shoping in the local supermarket. We marked that their weren't much billboards or advertising in general. Something we noticed during are whole stay in Iceland and we felt good with this fact.

    Now we got some necessary food in our trunk we drove to the free camping site at the lighthouse. Because we still had some time we decided to go for a dive in the Blue Lagoon. A geothermal spa provided by the hot water of the nearby power plant which uses the natural hot water to gain electricity. It's quite expensive and the most commercial place we visited during our trip, but it was relaxing that's for sure. Although it was a bit weird to shower totally naked before entering the pool. But it's an Icelands habbit when entering a pool.

    Our visit to the spa was about 2hrs and a half before we drove back to the free camping site. Setting up our new tent, still searching how to manage the luggage in the car and taking some nice pictures of the lighthouse we noticed the sun wasn't really going under.

    We brought some flashlights with us, but it seemed like we would never going to use those. So still in a quite lighting atmosphere we went to bed.
